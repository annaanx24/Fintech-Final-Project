What is Money?

Money is a form of exchange used to buy or sell things.

If I give you a sandwich for a juice box, that's an exchange.

In the real world, things can't be bought using food or drinks. Instead, you use coins, paper bills, and digital credit.

What is Saving and Investing?

Money can grow on trees! Well not really, but money can increase over time like a tree growing taller.

How can money grow? By saving and investing.

Saving is keeping money to let it add up to have more in the future.

If you get 25 cents per day in a week, that's 7 x 0.25 which is $1.75. You can buy a small candy bar with $1.75, but if you wait another week to save, you'll get $3.50 which can buy you a large candy bar and smaller candies!

Investing is keeping money to let it add up to have more in the future.

What is Credit and Debt?

If you borrow money from your parent or sibling, you need to pay it back!

What if you have a deadline and miss it? You will have to pay even more!

This is called credit, which comes from a lender (the person or place like a bank) and has interest if you don't pay back what you borrowed in time.

If you don't pay back in time and get interest, you're in debt.
